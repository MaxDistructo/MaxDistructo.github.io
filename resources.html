Cookware Section

The best equipped kitchen contains a variety of cooking surfaces, cookware shapes, and cookware sizes. To help you understand which type of cookware is right for you take a look at all the options.

Copper: This is highly conductive, and infact is the best material to make cookware from. It readily adapts to temperature changes as you cook. Copper is a soft metal that is susceptible to dings and scratches. It is relatively expensive and time consuming to maintain.

Aluminum: This is another highly conductive metal. Aluminum cookware heats and cooks evenly. Aluminum in its natural state is soft and will react with food acids.

Stainless Steel: This cookware is a very poor conductor of heat. This cookware tends to have hot and cold spots. It also warps under extremely hight heat. Stainless steel is often combined with other metals for durability. Stainless steel does not react with acidic foods.

Cast Iron: This cookwares is fairly heavy and conducts heat evenly. It heats slowly, but once it is hot it holds heat well. It can be used on top of the stove and in the oven. Drawbacks to cast iron are that it rusts, stains, and becomes pitted when exposed to air, moisture and certain foods.
